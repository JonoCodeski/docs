## Get Started in 5 Minutes

This quick start guide will have you reading and learning with MyLang Reader in just a few minutes.

<Info>
**Before you begin**: You can use MyLang Reader without an account, but creating one lets you save your progress, vocabulary, and content across devices.
</Info>

## Step 1: Access MyLang Reader

Visit [www.mylangreader.com](https://www.mylangreader.com) in your web browser. The application works on:

- **Desktop**: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge
- **Mobile**: iOS Safari, Android Chrome
- **Tablet**: All major browsers

<Tip>
For the best experience, we recommend using a desktop or tablet where you have more screen space for reading.
</Tip>

## Step 2: Choose Your Path

When you first open MyLang Reader, you have several options:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Start Reading Immediately" icon="play">
    Click "Continue as Guest" to start using the reader without creating an account
  </Card>
  <Card title="Create Account" icon="user-plus">
    Click "Sign Up" to save your progress and access all features
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Step 3: Set Your Languages (Optional)

If you create an account or continue as a guest, you'll be prompted to set:

- **Native Language**: Your first language (what you're fluent in)
- **Target Language**: The language you want to learn

<Note>
You can skip this step and set languages later, or change them anytime in your account settings.
</Note>

## Step 4: Import Your First Content

Once you're in the reader, click the **+ Import** button (usually a floating action button) and choose:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="News Article">
    1. Click **"Import from URL"**
    2. Paste any news article URL
    3. Click **"Import"**
    4. Wait for processing (usually 10-30 seconds)
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Upload File">
    1. Click **"Upload File"**
    2. Choose a PDF, ePub, Word doc, or text file
    3. Select source language if not detected
    4. Click **"Import"**
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="YouTube Video">
    1. Click **"Import from URL"**
    2. Paste a YouTube video URL
    3. Choose subtitle language
    4. Click **"Import"**
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Plain Text">
    1. Click **"Paste Text"**
    2. Copy and paste any text
    3. Set the source language
    4. Click **"Import"**
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

When you click a word, you'll see options to mark it as:
- **Learning**: You're studying this word
- **Known**: You know this word well
- **Difficult**: This word is challenging for you

### Basic Controls

- **Font size**: Adjust text size with + and - buttons
- **Translation mode**: Toggle to see translated text
- **Dual mode**: View original and translation side by side (Premium)

## Step 6: Practice What You've Learned

After reading, practice your vocabulary:

1. Go to the **Practice** section (card icon in navigation)
2. Click **"Start Mixed Session"**
3. Review flashcards for words you've encountered
4. Rate each card based on how well you knew it

<Info>
The spaced repetition system will automatically schedule reviews based on your performance.
</Info>

## Common First-Time Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What file types can I import?">
    MyLang Reader supports:
    - **Web content**: Any article URL
    - **Documents**: PDF, ePub, Word (.docx), plain text (.txt)
    - **Videos**: YouTube URLs with subtitles
    - **Text**: Direct copy and paste
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How accurate are the translations?">
    MyLang Reader uses advanced AI translation services. While generally accurate, always cross-reference important translations with dictionaries or native speakers.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I use this offline?">
    MyLang Reader requires an internet connection for importing content and translations. However, previously loaded content can be viewed offline (limited functionality).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is my data secure?">
    Yes, all data is encrypted and stored securely. We never share your personal information or learning data with third parties.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
