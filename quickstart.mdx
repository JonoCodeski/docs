## Get Started in 5 Minutes

This quick start guide will have you reading and learning with MyLang Reader in just a few minutes.

## Step 1: Access MyLang Reader

Visit [www.mylangreader.com](https://www.mylangreader.com) in your web browser. The application works on most browsers:

- **Desktop**: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge
- **Mobile**: iOS Safari or Chrome, Android Chrome
- A Native iOS app version is in development and will hopefully be on the Apple App Store in 2026.

</CardGroup>

## Step 2: Set Your Languages

- **Native Language**: Your first language (what you're fluent in)
- **Target Language**: The language you want to learn

## Step 3a: Import Your First Content

Once you're in the reader, click the **\+ Import** button and choose:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="News Article">
    1. Click **"Import from URL"**
    2. Paste any news article URL. Some paywalled sites may not work, in which case you could copy and paste text directly instead.
    3. Click **"Start Processing"**
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Upload File">
    1. Click **"Upload File"**
    2. Choose a PDF, ePub, Word doc, or text file
    3. Click "**Start Processing**"
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="YouTube Video">
    1. Click **"Youtube Video"**
    2. Paste a YouTube video URL
    3. Choose video source language
    4. Click **"Start Processing"**
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Plain Text">
    1. Click **"Paste Text"**
    2. Copy and paste any text
    4. Click **"Start Processing"**
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Step 3b: Importing from Feed

Feed allows you to enter any RSS URL for news articles and access them directly in MyLang Reader. Simply add sources via "Manage Sources" in the top right of the Feed page. You may also add Youtube channels to your feed, and access videos from those channels for direct imports.\
\
The Star icon on the right will favourite any item in feed for saving to your favourites lists for later. Otherwise items will update automatically as RSS and Youtube feeds update by their creators!

## Step 4: Start Reading, Watching, Listening and Learning!

Once your content is imported, you'll see it in the reader interface:

### Mark Word Familiarity

When you click a word, you'll see options to mark it as:

- **Learning**: You're studying this word (saves it to your list for practice!)
- **Known**: You know this word well

## Step 5: Practice Makes Perfect!!!

After reading, practice your vocabulary:

1. Go to the **Practice** section (card icon in navigation)
2. Click **"Study All"**
3. Review flashcards for words you've added to your vocabulary list.
4. Rate each card based on how well you knew it

<Info>
  The spaced repetition system will automatically schedule reviews based on your performance.
</Info>

## Common First-Time Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What file types can I import?">
    MyLang Reader supports:

    - **Web content**: Any article URL
    - **Documents**: PDF, ePub, Word (.docx), plain text (.txt)
    - **Videos**: YouTube URLs with subtitles
    - **Text**: Direct copy and paste
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How accurate are the translations?">
    MyLang Reader uses advanced AI translation services. While generally accurate, always cross-reference important translations with dictionaries or native speakers.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I use this offline?">
    MyLang Reader requires an internet connection for importing content and translations. However, previously loaded content can be viewed offline (limited functionality).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is my data secure?">
    Yes, all data is encrypted and stored securely. We never share your personal information or learning data with third parties.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
